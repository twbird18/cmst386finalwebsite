Sheperd's Pie

Ingredients

  • 3-4 Carrots
  • 2 Portobello Mushrooms (or mushrooms of your choice)
  • 1 Onion
  • 1 Green Pepper
  • 2 teaspoons minced Garlic
  • 1 Tablespoon Olive Oil
  • 1 Tablespoon Italian Seasoning
  • 2 Cups vegetable stock
  • 2 Tablespoons Soy Sauce
  • 1 Tablespoon Worcestershire
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon pepper
  • 4 large potatoes
  • 2 tablespoons vegan butter
  • 1/2 cup Soy milk
  • 1 tablespoon Nutritional yeast
  • 8 ounces Corn
  • 8 ounces Ground Beef substitute (I use Gardein)

Recipe

  1. Place Carrots, mushrooms, onions and green pepper in food processor until finely chopped.
  2. Heat large pan over medium heat with drizzle of olive oil. Bring diced potatoes to a boil in a large pot of water. Boil ~10 min until potatoes are cooked through.
  3. Add processed vegetables to pan and saut√© for 5 min.
  4. Add garlic, salt, pepper, Italian seasoning, and Beef Substitute to pan. Cook ~5 more min, stirring frequently.
  5. Add stock, soy sauce and Worcestershire sauce. Season with salt and pepper. Bring to a low simmer. Cook until reduced down ~10 min.
  6. Preheat oven to 400 F
  7. Drain potatoes, add nutritional yeast, salt, pepper, and butter. Mash with milk to desired consistency. Add milk slowly so you don't end up with potato soup.
  8. Place reduced meat mixture at bottom of small casserole dish. Top with frozen corn. Spread Mashed potatoes on top. Use a fork to create ridges in potatoes.
  9. Bake in oven for 25 min. Broil quickly before removing from oven if you like a crispy, crusty top.

Information

This recipe is great to add more vegetables to a kid's diet without them knowing.
You can swap in lentils for the beef or even use in addition for extra protein.
For a quicker version, use frozen mirepoix in the meat mix instead of fresh vegetables. Substitute a bag of your favorite frozen mixed vegetables for corn for a change.
This recipe is really quite versatile. For a more flavorful mixture, sub a cup of your favorite red wine for 1 cup of stock and reduce.
This recipe freezes well for ~3 months. Double your amounts and make 2, one for today and one for the future!
